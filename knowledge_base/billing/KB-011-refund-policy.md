# CloudDash Refund Policy

This article describes the situations in which CloudDash issues refunds, the timelines, and the limits of the Billing Agent's authority.

## Section 1 — Eligible for automatic refund

The Billing Agent is authorized to issue immediate refunds in the following situations:

1. **Duplicate charge**: a single billing period charged twice. See KB-012.
2. **Plan-mismatch**: you were charged for Enterprise but only have a Pro contract.
3. **Service outage credit**: if CloudDash had a documented outage exceeding the SLA window of your plan, automatic credits are issued the following month.
4. **Failed feature delivery**: if a feature listed in your plan tier was not available for ≥ 7 days due to a known CloudDash incident.

These refunds are processed within **1 business day** of confirmation. Refunds appear on your statement within 5–10 business days, depending on your card issuer.

## Section 2 — Requires manager review

The Billing Agent does NOT have authority for, and these escalate to a Billing Manager:

1. Refunds over **$1,000 USD** in any single dispute.
2. Refunds for charges older than the most recent **two billing cycles**.
3. Partial-month refunds for downgrades. CloudDash policy is to credit, not refund, the unused portion of a downgrade — see Section 4.
4. Goodwill refunds outside the eligibility list.
5. Refunds when there is also an active legal/contract dispute.

When the Billing Agent identifies a request that requires manager review, it automatically packages the conversation, customer profile, sentiment, and urgency context into a structured escalation ticket and routes it to a human Billing Manager. You will receive a confirmation immediately and a manager response within 1 business day.

## Section 3 — Not refundable

These categories are explicitly NOT refundable per our terms:

- Usage-based overage charges (you used the service, you pay for it).
- Charges from > 90 days ago (statute of limitations on disputes).
- Self-service plan changes you made by mistake (we credit toward future, but do not refund).

## Section 4 — Downgrade credit policy

When you downgrade mid-cycle:

- The downgrade takes effect at the **end of the current billing cycle** — you keep the higher-tier features until then.
- The unused portion of the higher-tier plan is **credited toward your next invoice**, not refunded to your card.
- This policy exists because most downgrades are temporary; the credit makes it cheaper to upgrade back later.

If you have closed your account and have credit remaining, contact support to request a refund — this requires manager approval.

## Section 5 — How to dispute

1. **Settings → Billing → Dispute a charge**.
2. Or reply to the invoice email.
3. Or in chat, request "refund for invoice INV-XXXX" — the Billing Agent will guide you.

Always include the invoice number for fastest processing.

## Section 6 — Sentiment and priority

If you mention urgency (e.g. "this is causing me hardship", "I need this resolved before Friday") or strong dissatisfaction (e.g. "I want to speak to a manager"), the system marks the ticket priority as **high** and routes appropriately. You can request a manager directly at any point.
